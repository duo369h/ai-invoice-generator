## 3. Page Sections

### Section 1 — Template Preview

A professional photography invoice covers:

- **Client and job reference** — tying the invoice back to the approved quote
- **Itemized deliverables** — what was shot and delivered, matching what was quoted
- **Amount already paid** — the deposit, clearly subtracted
- **Balance due** — the remaining amount, stated plainly
- **Payment terms** — due date and accepted payment methods

An invoice that doesn't reference the original quote forces the client to re-check what they agreed to — which is where payment delays usually start.

### Section 2 — Invoice Fields Photographers Need

Fields that consistently matter on a photography invoice:

- Invoice number and date issued
- Reference to the approved quote or contract
- Full breakdown of deliverables (matching the quote, not a summary)
- Deposit amount and date received
- Remaining balance and due date
- Late payment terms, if applicable
- Business details needed for the client's own records

Leaving any of these out is usually what triggers a client asking "wait, what's this for?"

### Section 3 — Common Invoice Problems

Photographers invoicing manually run into a recurring set of issues:

- **Invoice doesn't match the quote** — different numbers, confusing the client
- **Deposit not reflected** — client thinks they're being asked to pay twice
- **No clear due date** — payment gets deprioritized
- **No record of what's outstanding** — hard to know who still owes what, across multiple jobs

These are tracking problems, not pricing problems — and they tend to show up most once a photographer has more than a handful of jobs running at once.

### Section 6 — FAQ

**What's the difference between a photography quote and an invoice?**
A quote is the estimate a client approves before the job. An invoice is issued after approval to request payment, and reflects any deposit already collected.

**What should a photography invoice include?**
Client and job details, an itemized list of deliverables matching the quote, the deposit applied, the remaining balance, and a clear due date.

**How do I show a deposit on an invoice?**
List the deposit as a line item subtracted from the total, so the balance due is the only number the client needs to act on.

**Can an invoice be created directly from an approved quote?**
With Corvioz, yes — the invoice carries over the deliverables and pricing from the quote, so nothing needs to be re-entered.

**How do I keep track of unpaid invoices across multiple clients?**
Corvioz shows payment status per invoice, so you can see at a glance which jobs are paid, partially paid, or still outstanding.
