## 3. Page Sections

### Section 1 — Template Preview

A professional photography quote is built from five parts, in this order:

- **Project scope** — the type of shoot, date, location, and hours covered.
- **Deliverables** — number of edited images, formats, turnaround time, any albums or prints.
- **Pricing** — a clear breakdown, not a single lump sum, so clients see what they're paying for.
- **Usage rights** — where and how the client can use the images (personal, commercial, print, web).
- **Payment schedule** — deposit amount, due dates, and final payment terms.

When these five sections are missing or vague, quotes get questioned, delayed, or ignored.

### Section 2 — What Photographers Should Include in a Quote

Beyond the basics, quotes that hold up tend to include:

- A specific shoot date and estimated duration, not just "a session"
- The exact number of final images, not "a selection of the best shots"
- Whether raw/unedited files are included or excluded
- Travel fees, overtime rates, or reshoot policy, stated upfront
- A clear line separating the deposit from the total price
- An expiration date on the quote itself, so pricing doesn't stay open-ended

None of this needs to be complicated — it needs to be written down, every time, in the same format.

### Section 3 — Common Photography Quote Problems

Photographers running quotes manually tend to hit the same issues:

- **Unclear scope** — the client assumed something was included that wasn't
- **Price-only comparisons** — without context, a quote is just a number to shop against
- **Missing usage details** — disputes later over where images can be used
- **Deposit confusion** — clients unsure if the deposit is refundable or counts toward the total

These aren't pricing problems. They're formatting and follow-through problems.

### Section 6 — FAQ

**What should a photography quote template include?**
At minimum: project scope, deliverables, pricing breakdown, usage rights, and a payment schedule with deposit terms.

**Is a quote the same as an invoice?**
No. A quote is an estimate the client approves before work begins. An invoice is the formal request for payment, usually issued after approval or upon delivery.

**How do I handle deposits in a photography quote?**
State the deposit amount, whether it's refundable, and how it applies to the final balance — directly in the quote, not in a separate email.

**Should usage rights be included in the quote or a separate contract?**
Usage rights should at least be summarized in the quote so pricing expectations are clear; a full contract can cover further legal detail separately.

**Can I turn a quote into an invoice automatically?**
With Corvioz, an approved quote can be converted directly into an invoice, keeping scope, pricing, and deliverables consistent across both.
